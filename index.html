WILD LIFE
MOOSE
Moose (*Alces alces*), the largest members of the deer family, inhabit boreal forests across North America, Europe, and Asia. Males, distinguished by their broad antlers, can weigh up to 1,600 pounds. Adapted to cold climates, they thrive on a diet of leaves, bark, and aquatic plants. Moose are excellent swimmers and solitary by nature, except during the fall mating season. Predators include wolves, bears, and humans.

LION
Lions (*Panthera leo*), known as the "king of the jungle," are powerful carnivores native to Africa and parts of India. Males, identifiable by their majestic manes, can weigh up to 420 pounds. Living in social groups called prides, they hunt large prey like zebras and buffalo. Lions are apex predators but face threats from habitat loss and poaching. They are skilled hunters and exhibit cooperative behavior during hunts.

ELEPHANT
Elephants, the largest land animals, are found in Africa and Asia. African elephants, recognized by their large ears, can weigh up to 14,000 pounds. They live in matriarchal herds and are known for their intelligence, long trunks, and tusks. Elephants are herbivores, consuming grasses, leaves, and bark. They face threats from habitat loss and poaching for ivory. These social animals exhibit strong familial bonds and complex behaviors.

JAGUAR
Jaguars (*Panthera onca*), the largest cats in the Americas, inhabit rainforests and savannas from Mexico to Argentina. They can weigh up to 250 pounds and are known for their powerful build and striking coat with rosettes. Jaguars are solitary and apex predators, preying on deer, capybaras, and even caimans. They are excellent swimmers and climbers. Jaguars face threats from habitat loss and poaching for their fur.
